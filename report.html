1. Target Customer

1.1 Customer Segment

Our primary customers are small-to-mid-sized sellers on Shopee Taiwan (蝦皮購物) — typically sole proprietors or micro-businesses selling 50–500 SKUs across categories such as consumer electronics accessories, fashion, household goods, and health products. These sellers generate NT$50,000–NT$500,000 in monthly gross merchandise value (GMV), operate with thin margins, and lack the resources to hire dedicated pricing analysts.

1.4 Addressable Market

Shopee Taiwan's buyer-side app has accumulated 780,163 App Store ratings, placing it among Taiwan's top-3 shopping apps. Industry estimates suggest the seller:buyer ratio on C2C platforms is roughly 1:20, implying approximately 39,000 active Shopee Taiwan sellers. Targeting the top 10 % of sellers (those motivated enough to invest in tools) gives a beachhead of ~3,900 potential paying users — a market worth roughly NT$14 million / year at NT$299/month even at modest penetration.

2.1 Source A — PTT Forum Discussion Analysis

Methodology

We queried the PTT BBS search API (https://www.ptt.cc/bbs/{board}/search?q={keyword}) across two boards known for e-commerce discussion: e-shopping and BuyTogether. Six keywords were used: 蝦皮賣家、比價、降價通知、賣家工具、比價軟體、降價. The script is at demand_analysis/ptt_scraper.py.

Results

  • 58 unique threads retrieved after de-duplication
  • 40 threads (69 %)) flagged as containing a demand signal (posts about unfair pricing, sellers facing margin pressure, or requests for notification tools)
  • Representative threads: "[問題] 蝦皮免運新制讓賣家利潤更薄", "[閒聊] 東京著衣喊話蝦皮賣家搬家到雅虎", "[問題] 蝦皮賣家費率一直漲"
Interpretation: Threads reveal recurring seller complaints about platform fee increases, competitor dumping, and margin erosion — precisely the conditions that make price-monitoring tools valuable. Sellers already feel the pain; they just lack the tool to act on it quickly.

3.2 Data Sources & Ingestion

Price data is collected from Shopee Taiwan's public product API (https://shopee.tw/api/v4/item/get?itemid=<id>&shopid=<id>). This endpoint is unauthenticated and publicly accessible; it powers Shopee's own website. A Scrapy-based spider (scraper/shopee_spider.py) fetches each monitored product every 30 minutes via APScheduler. Price values are stored in the raw Shopee unit (price ÷ 100,000 = NT$) and converted on write.

Sellers add products to their watchlist by pasting a Shopee product URL into the dashboard. The system parses the URL with a regex (-i\.(\d+)\.(\d+)) to extract the shop ID and item ID, then immediately fetches the product name and price to confirm the link is valid before storing it.

3.5 Delivery

The customer interacts with the product through a Flask web dashboard running on port 5000. All data is fetched from a FastAPI backend (port 8000) that exposes a JWT-authenticated REST API. Price history charts are rendered client-side using Chart.js line charts showing 30-day competitor price trends against the seller's own price (displayed as a dashed reference line).

When Spark detects a price drop exceeding the seller's configured threshold (default 5 %), it immediately sends an email alert via Gmail SMTP, providing the product name, old price, new price, and percentage drop.

3.7 Infrastructure & Deployment

The full system is containerised with Docker Compose (8 services) and can be started with a single command: docker-compose up --build. For cloud deployment, the stateless services (API, Dashboard, Scraper) are deployed to Railway, with managed PostgreSQL and Redis provided by Railway plugins and Upstash for a managed Kafka cluster.

4. Bonus — Go-to-Market Difficulties

4.1 Data Acquisition: Legal and ToS Risk

Our scraper targets Shopee's public item API — the same endpoint the browser calls when loading a product page. However, Shopee's Terms of Service prohibit automated access, and the company could block our IP ranges or add bot-detection at any time. This is the most acute risk: if the data source is cut off, the entire product fails.

Mitigation strategies: (a) rotate residential proxies to reduce detection probability; (b) negotiate a data-sharing partnership with Shopee (likely available to verified third-party tool vendors via Shopee's Open Platform API); (c) pivot data source to sellers' own Shopee Open Platform credentials, which provide a legitimate, ToS-compliant path to their store's competitor data.

4.2 Trust and Adoption

Shopee sellers tend to be skeptical of third-party tools, particularly those that require them to connect their store data. Our system intentionally avoids asking for seller credentials in the MVP — sellers only provide competitor product URLs, not their own shop access. This reduces the perceived privacy risk and lowers the adoption barrier.

Building trust requires transparent uptime metrics, a clear privacy policy, and early-adopter reviews. The initial go-to-market channel is Shopee seller Facebook groups (e.g., 蝦皮賣家聯盟, ~80,000 members), where word-of-mouth and peer recommendations carry significant weight.

4.3 Cold-Start Problem

The product provides immediate individual value (it monitors your own watchlist) and has no network-effect dependency — a seller with a single competitor URL gets immediate utility. This avoids the classic chicken-and-egg cold-start problem. However, breadth of product category coverage depends on whether sellers in each category have added relevant competitor URLs; we must seed some curated watchlists per category to demonstrate value before broad adoption.

The most durable moat is data network effects: as more sellers use the system, aggregate anonymised pricing trends become a marketable dataset in their own right (premium tier feature: "What is the average price movement in your category this week?").

4.6 Competition from Shopee Itself

Shopee could build competitor price monitoring natively. However, doing so would create regulatory risk (monopoly concerns) and would disadvantage their largest brand-name sellers who prefer price opacity. Historically, marketplaces monetise seller tools as separate paid services (cf. Amazon Seller Central's Brand Analytics, priced at $39 USD/month). This indicates a viable commercial window before Shopee closes it.
